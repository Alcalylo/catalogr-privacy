Privacy Policy – Catalogr

Effective Date: January 19, 2026
Last Updated: February 21, 2026

Catalogr is a personal inventory and records management application designed to help users catalog belongings, documents, photos, and emergency-related information. We are committed to protecting your data and maintaining transparency about how it is handled.

Information We Collect

  • Email address for account creation and authentication
  • User-generated content including item details, notes, documents, photos, and videos
  • An internal user ID used to associate data with your account

Catalogr does not collect location data, device fingerprinting data, behavioral tracking data, or advertising identifiers.

How We Use Information

Your data is used solely to:

  • Authenticate and manage your account
  • Store and retrieve your inventory and uploaded content
  • Provide core application functionality
  • Maintain system security and integrity

We do not use your content for advertising, profiling, or AI model training.

Data Storage & Infrastructure

Catalogr stores structured data (such as item metadata and account information) in a managed PostgreSQL database. Uploaded files (photos, documents, videos) are stored separately in secure mounted storage.

All network communication is encrypted using HTTPS.

Data Retention & Account Deletion

You may delete your account at any time within the app. When an account is deleted:

  • All associated database records are permanently removed
  • All associated uploaded files are permanently deleted
  • Deletion is irreversible

We do not retain user content after account deletion beyond routine short-term infrastructure backups required for service stability.

Data Sharing

Catalogr does not sell, rent, or share personal data with third parties. We do not provide user data to advertisers.

We may disclose information only if required by law or valid legal process.

Tracking & Analytics

Catalogr does not use third-party analytics SDKs, advertising frameworks, or behavioral tracking systems.

User Responsibility

Users retain full ownership of the content they upload. By using Catalogr, you affirm that:

  • You have the legal right to upload the content you provide
  • The content does not violate applicable laws
  • The content does not infringe on third-party rights

Data Security

We implement industry-standard security practices including encrypted communication, authentication controls, and secure server infrastructure. No system can guarantee absolute security, but we take reasonable measures to protect user data.
